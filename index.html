Where your money lands now
Emergency and Health are kept as cash and filled first; any bucket you mark "Fill first" joins them and, once full, simply sits there — cash doesn't grow. Every other bucket is invested. While it fills, it takes its share of your monthly saving; as a bucket reaches its target, that share is re-divided among the buckets still being filled, keeping their original proportions. Here's the key part: once an invested bucket (like Travel or House) is full, it keeps growing, and everything it earns above its target spills into your Retirement fund — so a filled House quietly helps pay for retirement instead of sitting idle. Every target is the plain amount you entered, except Retirement, which is shown as its inflation-adjusted future value.

A plan is a direction, not a verdict.

If the number above feels heavy, take a breath. It will shift as your life does, and a target you can't reach today is something to grow toward — not a measure of your worth.

Money was never meant to be hoarded; it's a tool to live well. So prepare for the years ahead, yes — but don't forget to be fully alive in the one you're in.

Save wisely — then go live.
